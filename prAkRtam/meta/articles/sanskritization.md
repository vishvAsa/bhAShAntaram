Yet heavy Sanskritization of NIA languages in general is visible even in Diṅgaḷ poetry and the poems of Vidyāpati.
Sanskrit loans in NIA were normalized very early.

Entire expressions were loaned.
To use examples from Tulsīdās:

> suni prabhu parama sarala kapi bānī।  
"evam astu" taba kaheu bhavānī॥  
kahañ kosalādhīsa "dvau" bhrātā।  
dhanvī sakala-loka-bikhyātā॥ 

The phrase "evam astu" comes directly from Sanskrit. The word "dvau" (two) is not Prakrit. It is pure Sanskrit.

You won't find Arabic expressions like al-ġaraz, fī l-fūr, or even bi l-kull and fī l-hāl (commonly used Arabic expressions in modern Hindi-Urdu) in early NIA.
